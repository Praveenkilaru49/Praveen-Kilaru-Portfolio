Overview

This project applies Finite Element Analysis (FEA) to evaluate the thermal behavior of 316 Stainless Steel and AlSi10Mg during the FDM process using ANSYS. It analyzes temperature and heat flux distributions under steady-state and transient conditions.

Project Description

The project simulates layer-by-layer thermal deposition during the FDM process. ANSYS Workbench was used to capture thermal gradients and stresses that occur in 316SS and AlSi10Mg materials during metal 3D printing. Thermal sources mimicking print head motion, layer activation, and time-dependent behavior were modeled. Key observations included hotspots and residual stress formation impacting structural integrity.

We applied realistic boundary conditions, material properties, and thermal cycling to understand thermal-mechanical coupling. The simulation allowed for better prediction of print outcomes and insights into process optimization.

Tools Used

  • ANSYS Workbench
  • FDM 3D Printing Techniques
  • Simulation of Convection and Radiation Heat Transfer
  • Material Modeling (316 Stainless Steel, AlSi10Mg)

Skills Achieved

  • FEA Setup and Simulation
  • Transient Heat Transfer Analysis
  • Mesh Refinement and Post-processing
  • Engineering Insight on Material Performance

Conclusion

The FEA model provided detailed insight into the thermal response of metal materials under FDM. While 316SS demonstrated strong thermal stability, AlSi10Mg showed dynamic adaptability. These findings reinforce the role of simulation in optimizing additive manufacturing workflows.
