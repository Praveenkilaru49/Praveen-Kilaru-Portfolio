Design and Simulation of FDM Process

Overview

This project applies Finite Element Analysis (FEA) to evaluate the thermal behavior of 316 Stainless Steel and AlSi10Mg during the Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) process, using ANSYS for simulation of temperature and heat flux distributions under steady-state and transient conditions.

Project Description

This project focuses on performing a comprehensive Finite Element Analysis (FEA) of the Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) process utilized in Additive Manufacturing (AM). The main goal was to simulate the layer-by-layer deposition process using thermal transient analysis to understand the behavior of metallic materials—specifically 316 Stainless Steel (316SS) and AlSi10Mg—during 3D printing. Through simulations conducted in ANSYS Workbench, the study aimed to capture thermal gradients, residual stresses, and potential structural deformations that could arise due to rapid thermal cycles.

The model was developed using accurate material properties, boundary conditions, and a thermal source mimicking the print head’s motion. The simulation considered real-time temperature-dependent material behavior and layer activation sequences to represent the true FDM process. This allowed us to observe the heat distribution over time and assess its influence on the internal stresses that evolve in the printed parts. Post-processing of results revealed critical thermal hotspots and mechanical stress zones, offering valuable insight into optimizing print parameters.

This project strengthened our understanding of the relationship between thermal behavior and mechanical response in metal-based additive manufacturing. It emphasized the need for process optimization to enhance print quality and reliability. Key learnings included ANSYS-based thermal-mechanical coupling, mesh refinement strategies, and practical skills in interpreting FEA results to improve design and manufacturing practices for complex components.

Tools Used

  • ANSYS Workbench
  • FDM 3D Printing Techniques
  • Simulation of Convection and Radiation Heat Transfer
  • Material modeling of 316 Stainless Steel and AlSi10Mg

Skills Achieved

  • Finite Element Modeling & Simulation
  • Transient Heat Transfer Analysis
  • Post-processing of simulation results
  • Engineering judgment on thermal material performance

Conclusion

The FEA model successfully analyzed the thermal response of both materials in FDM, validating the effectiveness of simulation-driven design in AM. 316 SS proved ideal for thermal stability, while AlSi10Mg showed better adaptability through dynamic thermal performance.
